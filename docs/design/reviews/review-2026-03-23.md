### 1. Strategy Logic & P&L Edge Cases: The "Double Loss" Flaw
The theoretical P&L analysis in `TRADING_MECHANICS.md` is mathematically correct for **monotone strike chains** (e.g., `BTC > 65k` and `BTC > 68k`). The pricing structure of these chains natively prevents crossing, ensuring a true "safe zone" where both legs win, and guaranteeing that at most one leg can lose at the extremes. 

**However, `trader.py` fails to restrict pairing to monotone chains.** It blindly pairs any event with two opposing tails, including mutually exclusive multi-way markets (e.g., tournament brackets or primary elections).
- **The Risk:** If you execute a pair on a mutually exclusive market—buying YES on the favorite (Team A at 90¢) and NO on a longshot (Team B at 95¢)—you are exposed to a catastrophic double loss. If Team B wins, your YES A leg loses (A didn't win) AND your NO B leg loses (because B won, so NO B pays $0). 
- **Severity: High.** This directly violates the documented "Key property: a loss only hits one leg." The `BLOCKED_SERIES` hardcodes a few known multi-way markets, but this is a structural vulnerability. The scanner must programmatically distinguish between monotone chains and multi-way markets before pairing.

### 2. Calibration Methodology: Severe Pseudo-Replication
The adaptive bucketing logic in `CALIBRATION_METHODOLOGY.md` suffers from a critical statistical flaw that will lead to vastly overconfident edge estimates.
- **The Issue:** The methodology pools hourly candle data and equipartitions them into buckets of `N=200` without deduplicating by market. Because a single market can exist for days, it can easily contribute 100+ highly autocorrelated hourly observations to a single time bucket.
- **The Impact:** A bucket of 200 observations might actually represent only 2 or 3 distinct markets. Since all hourly quotes from a single market perfectly correlate to the exact same final resolution, the true sample size (effective $N$) is dangerously low. The claimed "SE ≈ 2.1%" is invalid; the edge estimates will be highly volatile and overfit to the outcomes of a tiny handful of specific markets that happened to fall in that window.
- **Actionable Fix:** The bucketing must be bounded by *distinct markets*, not raw observations. You should either take one observation per market per bucket or apply sample weighting to prevent long-lived markets from artificially inflating the statistical confidence of the edge.

### 3. Implementation Bugs in `trader.py`

**A. Execution Atomicity Failure (High Severity)**
The strategy is designed to "Place paired orders atomically — both legs or neither." However, the loop in `place_pairs` executes sequentially:
```python
for i, opp in enumerate(legs):
    oo = _try_place_one(...)
```
If the first leg is successfully placed but the second leg fails (e.g., API rejection, spread crosses, or network timeout), the loop simply moves on. The first leg remains on the order book completely unprotected and unpaired.
*Fix:* Implement a rollback catch. If `_try_place_one` returns `None` for the second leg, immediately issue a `cancel_order` request for the first leg.

**B. Destructive State Management / P&L Tracking (Medium Severity)**
In `sync_state()`, the `state.open_orders` dictionary is completely overwritten by `client.get_orders(status="resting")`.

*Fix:* Do not discard orders from state just because they are no longer resting. Maintain a persistent `state.filled_orders` dictionary to ensure reliable P&L calculation at settlement.

### 2. Edge Cases and Undocumented Risks

**A. Correlation breakdown in binary events (Severity: Medium)**

The docs acknowledge that for binary events (sports), "both sides are correlated" (line 143), but they don't quantify this. If Team A is heavily favored, both longshot tails might be unlikely, but they're *mutually exclusive* outcomes. The fee optimization in `optimal_quantity` (lines 157-180) uses the intended bid price, but Kalshi fees are computed at fill time based on the actual matched price. If a resting order fills at a better price, the fee changes. The code doesn't account for this variance.
