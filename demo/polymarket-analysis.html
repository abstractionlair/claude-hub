💡 Trading Implications

Contrarian Strategy

Fade large moves, especially on longshots. The combination of mean reversion and favorite-longshot bias suggests:

  • When longshots (<30%) spike upward, sell (buy "No")
  • When favorites (>70%) drop sharply, buy
  • Very large moves (>10%) have the strongest reversion tendency
  • Effect is consistent across up and down moves

Expected Returns (Before Fees)

Based on 13.5% excess reversion on very large moves:

  • Naive strategy: 13.5% edge per trade
  • Polymarket fees: 2% (maker), more for taker
  • Net edge: ~10-11% per trade
  • Opportunity frequency: ~1095 very large moves in 500 markets (2.2 per market)

⚠️ Limitations

Data Quality Issues

  • Sports betting dominated: 87% of sample (870/1000 observations in 50-60% bucket)
  • 12-hour fidelity: Resolved markets limited to 12hr intervals; momentum may exist at shorter timescales
  • Small sample in tails: Only 60 observations in 10-40% range, 20 in 70-90%
  • Price timing: Using mid-history prices may not reflect typical trader entry points

Next Steps for Validation

  • Collect active markets with minute-level data to test if reversion persists at shorter timescales
  • Focus on political/crypto markets with longer durations and more volatility
  • Break out analysis by market category (sports vs political vs crypto)
  • Test actual trading strategy with paper trading to account for slippage and timing
  • Compute statistical significance and confidence intervals
